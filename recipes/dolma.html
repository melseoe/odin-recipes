Dolma Recipe

Description

Dolma is a dish that embodies the warmth and tradition of Turkish hospitality. These stuffed peppers, filled with a flavorful mix of ground meat, rice, and spices, are slow-cooked to perfection, releasing an irresistible aroma. Every bite is a taste of home, evoking memories of family gatherings and celebrations. Often served with creamy yogurt, Dolma brings comfort and connection, uniting generations through the timeless art of sharing food made with love.

Ingredients

For the filling:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 250g ground beef
  • 1 large onion
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp dried mint
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/4 cup parsley
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1/2 lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup pine nuts
  • 1/4 cup currants or raisins

For the peppers:

  • 6-8 medium bell peppers (green, red, or yellow)
  • Olive oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 1-2 slices of lemon

For serving:

  • Yogurt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the peppers: Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds carefully, leaving the peppers intact. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the filling: In a large pan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until soft and translucent, about 5-7 minutes.
    Add the ground meat and cook until browned.
    Stir in the rice, paprika, dried mint, thyme, pine nuts (if using), and currants (if using). Season with salt and pepper.
    Add the grated tomatoes, tomato paste (if using), and a small amount of water (about 1/4 cup) to help the rice cook. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes, just enough to soften the rice slightly but not fully cook it.
    Stir in the fresh parsley and lemon juice. Remove from heat and allow the filling to cool slightly.


  3. Stuff the peppers: Carefully stuff each pepper with the filling, pressing it down gently to ensure it's packed. Leave a little room at the top for the rice to expand during cooking. Repeat with the remaining peppers.


  4. Arrange the dolmas: Place the stuffed peppers in a large pot, standing them up tightly against each other.
    Drizzle the stuffed peppers with olive oil and add water (or broth) to cover the peppers about halfway. You can add a slice of lemon on top for extra flavor.
    Place a heavy plate or lid on top to keep the peppers submerged during cooking.


  5. Cook the dolmas: Bring the pot to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer and cook for about 45-60 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the rice inside is fully cooked.


  6. Let the dolmas rest for 10-15 minutes before serving. Serve warm with a side of yogurt for added richness.

Enjoy your Dolma! A delicious and satisfying dish that brings comfort and warmth to any meal!
